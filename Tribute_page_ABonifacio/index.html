Andres Bonifacio

The father of Philippine Revolution

Timeline of Andres Bonifacio y de Castro life:

  • 1863 - Born in Tondo, Manila
  • 1877 - Andres becomes an orphan. He was orphaned and he had to take care of his younger siblings. Bonifacio made canes and paper fans to support his family.
  • 1892 The meeting. Bonifacio and others officially "founded" the Katipunan, or in full, Kataas-taasan, Kagalang-galang na Katipunan ng mga Anak ng Bayan ("Highest and Most Respected Society of the Country's Children"; Bayan can also denote community, people, and nation).
  • 1896 Secret Gathering. Eluding an intensive manhunt, Bonifacio called thousands of Katipunan members to a mass gathering in Caloocan, where they decided to start their uprising.
  • 1896 The Battle. On August 30, 1896, Bonifacio personally led an attack on San Juan del Monte to capture the town's powder magazine and water station (which supplied Manila). On November 7, 1896, Bonifacio led an assault on San Mateo, Marikina and Montalban. The Spanish were forced to retreat,
  • 1897 The death of The Father of Philippine Revolution. The Bonifacio brothers were executed on May 10, 1897, in the mountains of Maragondon.
  • 1918 Bonifacio's bones. The American colonial government of the Philippines mounted a search for Bonifacio's remains in Maragondon. A group consisting of government officials, former rebels, and a man reputed to be Bonifacio's servant found bones which they claimed were Bonifacio's in a sugarcane field on March 17, 1918.
